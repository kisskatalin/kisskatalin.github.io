Mastercard Billpay

Client: Mastercard • Duration: December 2020 – March 2021, July 2021 – August 2021 • Tools: Photoshop, Figma, FigJam, paper and pen

The project was made for UI Academy course.

Problem Statement

In Hungary, a huge amount of bills are paid through yellow checks at post offices every year while the online payment is also there. There isn't any aggregator application on the market. Service providers could use their own channel, but it would have maintenance and development costs while the customers would see different user interfaces all the time when they pay their bills to each provider. This would reduce the customer experience as a whole.

Solution

Mastercard builds on the existing Bill Pay Exchange and releases Billpay. The application is available to all the service providers in Hungary to reach their customer base. The customers can pay their bills through the mobile application which also provides the possibility of recurring payments with debit card or bank account.

Target Audience

People who want to keep all their payments in one place. However, many of them do not trust banks enough to stop using yellow checks.

Persona

Based on the brief given by UI Academy, I created a persona who has the same characteristics.

Marika (54)
Marika is a cashier in Szeged, Hungary. Since she works on weekdays, she only has time to pay her checks on Saturday morning. When her salary arrives to her bank account, she immediately picks it up from the ATM. She always pays with cash, only uses her debit card when she spends a larger amouth or doesn't have enough money in her wallet. Marika owns a smartphone, her most used applications are Facebook, Viber, email and browser.

User Journey Map

Phase 1 1. Sees the ad on Facebook, but ignores it at first. 2. Sees the TV commercial for the application and starts to think of. 3. Visits mastercard.hu to see more info on the application. Phase 2 4. Sees the mobile application is available for free on App Store. 5. Checks out if there is any other payment application 6. Checks out the applications by Mastercard and reads their user reviews 7. Downloads it. Phase 3 8. Creates an account. 9. Searches for services. Sees that the most of her services are available on the app. Phase 4 10. Adds her client ID to the app and sees she has unpaid bills 11. Plans if she has time to pay her bills with checks at the post office 12. Decides that she is too busy to go to the post office and is willing to pay her bills on the app Phase 5 13. Reads the privacy policy and saves her debit card on the app 15. Is notified that the addition was successful 16. Reviews the checkout 17. Push the button to pay the bills Phase 6 18. Is notified that the payment was successful, checks out her account balance 19. Checks out if she has more unpaid bills

Design: Concepts

My main goal with the design was to leave the user in the comfort zone. Since this group is not the most familiar with modern technology and their trust in banks are very uncertain, I wanted to create a simple and clear user interface with a few but necessary colors that keep them focused.

Low-fidelity wireframe

Design System

The interface was designed based on the official Mastercard design book. Mastercard has a well-known trademark and I wanted to make a design which is loyal to the brand, that's why orange was chosen as the primary color which represents the brand pretty well, so do the inputs, checkboxes and buttons with rounded corners.
