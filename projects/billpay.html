Mastercard Billpay App

Duration → December 2020 – March 2021

Tools → Photoshop, Figma, paper and pen

The project was made for UI Academy design course.

Billpay is a mobile application available to all the service providers in Hungary to reach their customer base. You can pay your bills via mobile and can make recurring payments with debit card or bank account.

Problem Statement

In Hungary, a huge amount of bills are paid through yellow checks at post offices every year while the online payment has been available for over a decade. There isn't any aggregator application on the market.

Service providers could use their own channel, but it would have maintenance and development costs while the customers would see different user interfaces all the time when they pay their bills to each provider. This would reduce the customer experience as a whole.

Target Audience

People who want to keep all their payments in one place. However, many of them do not trust banks enough to stop using yellow checks.

Based on the brief given by UI Academy, I created a persona who has the same characteristics:

Marika (54)
Marika is a cashier in Szeged, Hungary. Since she works weekdays, she only has time on Saturday morning to go to the post office to pay her bills. She always pays with cash, only uses her debit card when she spends a larger amouth or doesn't have enough money in her wallet. Marika owns a smartphone, her most used applications are Facebook, Viber, email and browser.

Concepts

My main goal with the design was to leave the user in the comfort zone.

Since this group is not the most familiar with modern technology and their trust in banks are very uncertain, I wanted to create a simple user interface with a few but necessary colors that keep them focused.

Low-Fidelity Wireframe

Design System

I created a design system based on the official brand book.

I chose orange as the primary color because it represents Mastercard very well. I wanted to strengthen the brand with the design system because Mastercard is a well-known trademark which could help the users to build trust.

High-Fidelity Prototype

Interactions

A flow with over twenty screens from searching for a service through adding it to paying the bills.

Impact Overview

Users tested my high-fidelity prototype while I was working on it and modified it where they run into difficulties.

In the final prototype, they could complete all the tasks that I asked for, like searching for and adding a service to the app, completing a bill payment, scanning the QR code from a check.

The users liked that the app tries to be loyal to the brand which meant safety for them in the sense that they weren't afraid to add their debit cards to the app.

Overall, the app gives the opportunity to reduce the number of the physical check payments.
