Problem Statement

In Hungary, a huge amouth of bills are paid through yellow checks at the post offices every year while the online payment is also there.

Solution

There isn't any aggregator application on the market, so Mastercard builds on the existing Bill Pay Exchange and releases Billpay. The application is available to all the service providers in Hungary, so they don't have to use their own channel which would have costs and the customers don't have to use different interfaces which may reduce the customer experience. It also provides the possibility of recurring payments with a debit card or a bank account.

Target audience

45+ year-old women who don't trust online banking, that's why they prefer to pay the bills through checks.

Design: Concepts & Sketching

The low-fidelity wireframe was given by UI Academy. Based on that, I started sketching my ideas for the high-fidelity wireframe.

While working as a front-end web developer, I watched this audience and got to know them better. I even made some researches then that I could re-use in this project.

The client wanted a simple layout for the application. According to their research, the target audience uses basic mobile applications (email, browser, Facebook and Viber), so knowing that fact, my main point was to make it clear with a limited color range. Also, showing the brand on the screens would put the user in the comfort zone.
