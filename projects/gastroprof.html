Gastro Prof ERP

Tools → Figma, Adobe XD, Microsoft Teams, paper and pen

Gastro Prof is a web-based ERP system which is used by Matusz-Vad Zrt. to collect, store and manage data from business activities.

Goal

To make the business processes faster by less complicated and more logical interfaces.

My main project at Matusz-Vad Zrt. was to redesign the Java-based ERP system to a web structure. Every department of the company, from warehouse to sales, uses the ERP, so all processes are integrated into one place. I wanted to keep this control and the basics of the system in the new design while removing all redundancy, merging identical processes to reduce the huge number of modules and creating a user-friendly and responsive interface.

Problem Statement

The ERP was functionally uncoordinated and looked very outdated.

The company employees complained about the lack of multitasking capability, the difficulty of searching and useless functions. Other problems I noticed were loading slowness, incorrect button and label titles, spelling mistakes, confusing error messages, fonts and colors.

Research

I was in touch with SQL developers, employees and managers.

I visited the departments and spent hours with the employees in the office and the warehouse. I watched them and tried to understand what they do, and how the real-life results get added to the ERP system.

Sketch

I created many versions of the ERP on paper.

One of my early findings was to simplify the menu, separate the search from the menu and add tabs to the window.

Differences between the old and new

As I wrote earlier, I decided to keep the way the functions work because the users are familiar with them and all they needed to be more organized. However, I had to eliminate pieces that didn't fit into the flow (e.g. an active Save button when the user hasn't interacted with the interface yet).

Similarities between the old and new

As I wrote earlier, I decided to keep the way the functions work because the users are familiar with them and I didn't find any problem in this part of the system, they just had to be more organized in flows.

In the old system, there were two types of error messages: one in a pop-up window, two on the left bottom which appeared when the user didn't fill out an input field correctly. In my solution, a window popped up when the system asks for save before leaving, and a message shown on the bottom when there is a success/warning/informational/error.

The users liked to bookmark modules to reach them easier, so I moved that function into the new design with improvement. In the old system, before opening it, you had to right click onto the title of the module to add it to the favorites, but it was unable to remove due to lack of programming. In the new one, you click on the icon next to the title and the module is added to the bookmarks without leaving

Impact Overview

Matusz-Vad Zrt. is one of the most valuable food sales companies in Hungary, so having a modern ERP system is very needed.

Being in touch with the employees through the design processes was effective. To me, it was easier to understand the operation of the system and get to know their problems by consulting with them.
