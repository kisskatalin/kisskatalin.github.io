Finding Jeans That Fit

Duration: August 2021 • Tools: Photoshop, Figma, paper and pen

The project was made for UX Challenge task.

Problem Statement

Shopping for jeans and other form-fitting apparel online could be time-consuming and frustrating. It's hard to compare between different styles, and it's hard to know for sure how they will fit. It's a disappointing experience when you have to return an item that doesn't fit quite right. How can you help customers select the style of jeans that they’re looking for in the right size?

Solution

Research

I wanted to learn about what the customers buy at fashion stores online.

Research questions

  • Do they buy clothes online?
  • Do they buy jeans online?
  • How satisfied are they while browsing the selection of jeans?

User Research Plan

Participants
  • Users who buy clothes often but are afraid to buy jeans
5 participants were interviewed about which type of clothes they buy and where.
In-person semi-structured interview questions
  1. How often do you buy clothes online? (Are they experienced customers?)
  2. Do you buy clothes in stores or online more often? (Do they trust in online stores?)
  3. When you buy something online, why do you prefer that way? (What motivates them to shop?)
  4. What type of clothes do you buy online? (Do they buy jeans?)
  5. Where do you buy clothes online? (Which stores are they satisfied with?)
  6. Have you ever had a size that wasn't good? Did you return it? (Did they use this service?)

All the info given helped me to create a better customer experience.

Analysis

Key Findings

  1. None of the participants buy jeans online

    They find it risky.

    "It's hard to guess if it fits ot not."
    "I buy everything but pants."

  2. 1 out of 5 participants shop more often online than at a store

    "The store is far away. I usually find the cloth cheaper online."
    "In terms of buying clothes, stores are better."

  3. None of the participants return their non-fitting clothes

    "I always order from the same places, so I know my sizes already. If it doesn't fit, I rather sell it."
    "If they don't fit, I give them to my friends."

Competitive Analysis

Before I started designing, I visited three fashion websites to check out what logic and interface they provide when they sale jeans.

Mother Denim has the most professional interface of them all. They use a video of the model going around in the jeans that confirms the customer's decision. However, the information on the jeans could be more detailed.
